Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 4, 2026

Your privacy matters to us. This Privacy Policy explains how Debt Success collects, uses, protects, and shares your personal information in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA).

1 Introduction

Debt Success ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your personal information and respecting your privacy. As a registered debt counseling service in South Africa, we comply with all applicable laws, including POPIA.

This Privacy Policy applies to all personal information we collect through our Client Portal and other services.

2 Information We Collect

We collect the following types of personal information:

Personal Identification Information

  • Full name and surname
  • South African ID number
  • Email address
  • Phone number (landline and/or mobile)
  • Physical and postal address

Financial Information

  • Debt counseling status
  • Financial Wellness Programme (FWP) progress and current step
  • Payment information (when you upgrade to paid services)
  • Financial documents you submit through our portal

Communication Data

  • Feedback forms and questionnaires
  • Questions and support requests
  • Email correspondence
  • Programme responses and insights

Technical Information

  • Login timestamps
  • Session information (for security purposes)
  • Browser type and device information (automatically collected)

3 How We Use Your Information

We process your personal information for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery: To provide debt counseling services and administer the Financial Wellness Programme
  • Progress Tracking: To monitor your progress through the FWP steps and provide personalized support
  • Communication: To send you programme materials, updates, and respond to your questions
  • Support: To contact you when you request assistance or check-ins
  • Improvement: To analyze feedback and improve our services
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with South African financial services regulations
  • Payment Processing: To process payments for upgraded services (via Paystack)

4 International Data Transfer

Important Notice: Your personal information may be processed and stored on servers located outside of South Africa, including in the United States.

We use the following international service providers:

  • Firebase (Google LLC): United States - for authentication, security, and session management
  • Zapier Inc.: United States - for data management, workflow automation, and secure data storage
  • Paystack: For payment processing (when you purchase upgraded services)

Safeguards for International Transfers

We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place through:

  • Encryption of all data in transit (HTTPS/TLS)
  • Encryption of data at rest on servers
  • Access controls and multi-factor authentication
  • Regular security audits and monitoring
  • Compliance with international data protection standards (ISO 27001, SOC 2)
  • Contractual agreements requiring service providers to protect your data

Your Consent: By using our Client Portal and checking the POPIA consent checkbox during login, you explicitly consent to the international transfer of your personal information as described above.

5 Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your personal information:

  • Encryption: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS
  • Authentication: Enterprise-grade Firebase Authentication with secure session management
  • Access Controls: API key restrictions ensure only authorized applications can access data
  • Monitoring: Continuous security monitoring and logging of access attempts
  • Password Security: Passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text
  • Regular Updates: Security patches and updates are applied promptly

While we take all reasonable steps to protect your information, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but are committed to maintaining the highest standards.

6 Your Rights Under POPIA

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

Right to Access

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you. We will provide you with a copy of your data within 30 days of your request.

Right to Correction

You have the right to request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal information. You can update most of your information directly through the Client Portal.

Right to Deletion

You have the right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to:

  • Legal requirements to retain financial services records
  • Ongoing contractual obligations
  • Legitimate business interests

Right to Object

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal information for certain purposes, such as marketing communications.

Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to request that we restrict processing of your personal information in certain circumstances.

Right to Withdraw Consent

You have the right to withdraw your consent to data processing at any time. However, this may affect our ability to provide services to you.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer using the contact information at the end of this policy.

7 Data Retention

We retain your personal information for the following periods:

  • Active Clients: For the duration of your participation in the Financial Wellness Programme, plus 5 years after completion
  • Inactive Clients: As required by South African financial services regulations (typically 5-7 years)
  • Communication Records: Email correspondence and feedback forms are retained for 5 years
  • Financial Records: Payment records are retained for 7 years as required by law

After the retention period expires, we securely delete or anonymize your personal information.

8 Sharing Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share your information with:

Service Providers

  • Technology providers (Firebase, Zapier, Paystack) who help us deliver our services
  • Email service providers for sending programme communications

Legal Requirements

  • When required by law or court order
  • To comply with financial services regulations
  • To protect our rights, property, or safety, or that of others

Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your personal information may be transferred to the new owner, subject to the same privacy protections.

9 Cookies and Tracking

Our Client Portal uses minimal tracking technology:

Essential Cookies

  • Authentication Cookies: Firebase session cookies to keep you logged in (essential for functionality)
  • Security Cookies: To prevent unauthorized access and protect against fraud

What We Don't Use

  • ❌ Third-party advertising cookies
  • ❌ Analytics tracking (we respect your privacy)
  • ❌ Social media tracking pixels
  • ❌ Cross-site tracking

You can control cookies through your browser settings, but disabling essential cookies may prevent you from using the portal.

10 Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child without parental consent, we will take steps to delete that information.

11 Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors.

When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy
  • Notify you via email (to the address on file)
  • Display a prominent notice in the Client Portal
  • Request your renewed consent where required by law

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.
